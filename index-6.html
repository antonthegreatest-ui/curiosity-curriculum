How the curriculum works

Two phases, one exploration

Young people move through engagement first, building the trust and routine that makes real learning possible. Then the curiosity curriculum begins: self-directed, personally meaningful, and accredited.

1
Phase One

The Engagement Curriculum

Built on the PLIMS framework, this curriculum meets young people where they are. The goal isn't academic. It's attendance, relationship and the careful rebuilding of trust in education.

Built on PLIMS
2
Phase Two

The Curiosity Curriculum

For young people who are attending regularly and ready for something of their own. Every young person identifies a personal project, anything that genuinely interests them, and earns an AQA Unit Award certificate for it.

What is PLIMS?

PLIMS is a new piece of pedagogy developed for SEN and SEMH settings. It provides a structured framework for capturing and responding to individual learner needs, drawing on EHCP targets and professional reports to inform a truly personalised curriculum. It underpins both phases.

The philosophy

Built on a simple belief

Every young person has something they care about. Our job isn't to decide what that should be. It's to create the conditions where they can find it, name it, and pursue it.

These curricula were built in a SEMH setting, co-authored by young people, over years of iteration, representing the end product of what works to engage and energise a young person to explore their own personal interests.

Relationship before curriculum. Young people need to feel safe before they can learn. The engagement phase earns the right to teach.
Self-identified interest only. A project chosen by a young person is a project they will protect and sustain. The topic has to be theirs.
Every young person, a certificate. Success means every young person has a named personal project and a real qualification to show for it.
Open and free, always. These tools exist to be shared. No paywalls, no sign-ups. Take what's useful, adapt it, pass it on.
